       <h2> Aim High</h2>

     <body>

           <p>When I was thirteen years old my brother came home from Germany (he was in the Army) and purchase a brand new Camaro.  That is when I knew that the  military was going to be my chosen profession. I seen first hand how it can change your life. Military was going to allow me to travel the world. Military would allow me to retire before forty. Military was going to provide financial security for my family.<br>

     <h3>Focused Teen</h3><br>

<img src = “fort worth.PNG”>   <br>     

<p>I’m originally from Fort Worth, Texas<b>  and had never been out of the state and only a few times out of the city.      With my brother coming home from Germany and all the stories and pictures he showed me. I knew that the world as truly bigger than the small part that I had seen. Stories of the castles, different food, and the new cultures that he experienced put a drive in me to achieve this goal. He had traveled to Germany, Belgium, Paris to name a few of the places. In my military career I travel to Oklahoma, Germany, Illinois and Maryland.  I didn’t see the world but I got out of Texas.   </p> <br>

<a href=“https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas”> Visit Wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas!</a> <br>  

  <h4>Career Minded</h4> <br>

       <p>I started researching each branch of the military for retirement  requirement which was my ultimate desire.  The standard was to complete 20 years of active duty in good standing. I figured that I would go directly out of high school therefore I would be thirty-nine or forty at the oldest.  With this plan I would be able to retire by the age of forth and never have to work again. I then researched the requirement to joining each branch and the Air Force seem to offer better opportunities and  accommodation which was important to me. Air Force retirement requirement wasn’t as stringent as the other branches. You where able to retire as an E-5.  I kept in constant communication with my brother on what he recommended would be best for me.</p>  <br>

<a href+”https://militarybenefits.info/military-ranks-insignia-charts/“> Visit Military Benefits.Info </a> <br>

    <h5> All About Family</h5> <br>

    <p>I realized that I could make almost as much money a month with all the incentives the military offered than my parents made.  The additional money could allow me to save and become financially independent by the retirement.  I would be able to assist my parents with the basics so they wouldn’t have to work so hard and have time with my special needs family member.</p>

  <6>Gained More Than I Expected </6> <br>

           <p>I seen different parts of the world, not as much as I would have liked. Stayed over twenty years in the military because I enjoyed it some much. Most important I had a family of my own and the military provided me the opportunity to ensure my family was is still taken care of.  I am very appreciative of everything I’ve achieved with the decision I made as a thirteen year old girl.  <br>
